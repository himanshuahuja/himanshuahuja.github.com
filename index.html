How about the professional bit?


I studied marketing at university but quickly realised that studying marketing is actually just the beginning. Its the beginning of the understanding that goes behind selling something.

In this superbly connected world that we live in today has a lot of innovation and lots of products but the one thread that ties them all together is that they all need customers. Without paying customers, a product is at best a great concept.

If you think I'm exaggerating, consider the following quote by Thomas J Watson - "Nothing happens until someone sells something".

This is where I come in. I have worked with several companies and brought them several clients and several crores in business.

I am able to sell so well only due to one reason - I never aim to sell, I aim to solve a customer problem. If there is no problem, I never push to sell but the interesting thing is that I have yet to find a prospect who doesn't have a problem. There is always a problem if you look hard enough and I always look harder than anyone else.

I hope this gives you a good idea about me and if you want to see further then please check out my CV page.
